About

The Pickup tracks emerging buyers and new forms of entertainment financing — the companies actively writing checks, and what they are buying.

Coverage

Microdrama platforms, creator studios, brand-funded entertainment, FAST channels, digital platforms, new production and distribution models, and legacy studios entering these emerging areas.

What we track
01

Confirmed deals — acquisitions, commissions, and partnerships.

02

Developing signals — executive hires, casting activity, platform expansions.

03

Legacy crossovers — traditional studios entering emerging formats.

04

Buyer mandates — what each buyer appears to want, based on public evidence.

Editorial standards

Every record separates verified facts from interpretation. We never present speculation as confirmed reporting. When we don't know something, we say so.

An evidence-tier system and confidence ratings signal how much certainty backs each claim. Inferred mandates are labeled as such.

How to read a record
Record class

Confirmed Deal, Developing Signal, or Context.

Strategic tags

Legacy Crossover, Vertical, Creator-led, FAST, and Brand-funded. A record may carry several.

Evidence tier

The strength and type of support for the material claim.

Mandate confidence

High, Medium, or Low confidence in what the evidence says the buyer wants.

Route state

Not researched, Underway, Verified, Likely, or Researched — none identified.

Evidence classes

The four tiers are classes of evidence, not a weakest-to-strongest ladder — T4, direct verification, is the strongest signal we publish. Where a signal was discovered is recorded separately from the strength of the evidence supporting it. A job board or programming grid is a discovery lane, not an automatic evidence grade.

T1
Primary public evidence

Official announcements, filings, company pages, investor materials, and on-the-record statements.

T2
Corroborated reporting

Independent, established reporting with attributable sourcing.

T3
Operational signal

Job and casting activity, app or catalog changes, programming changes, and observable patterns that require interpretation.

T4
Direct verification

A material claim confirmed directly by a buyer or source. Identity remains confidential unless disclosure is authorized.

Who it's for

Working entertainment professionals — writers, producers, directors, agents, managers, financiers, and executives — who need to know where the money is moving and whether there's a legitimate professional action they can take.
