# Role: Social Cutter

**Mode:** AI drafts; human reviews for no more than 10 minutes before scheduling.

**Cadence:** Tuesday after the Monday free edition publishes. Optional drip posts may run Wednesday through Saturday.

**Governing rules:** [Desk Constitution](./desk-architecture.md), especially Rules 1, 5, 7, 9, 10, and 11.

## Reads

- The signed free edition only.
- No paid edition, Airtable view, raw record, candidate, or research note.

If information did not survive the signed free-edition gate, it cannot appear on social.

## Writes

- One X thread, normally four to eight posts depending on the week's material.
- One LinkedIn post, normally 250–450 words.
- Optionally, up to three standalone X posts for the remaining signed items.
- No database writes.

## Never

- Never introduce a fact, number, conclusion, or source absent from the signed free edition.
- Never use deal verbs for signals.
- Never claim the trades missed an item unless the signed edition contains an approved coverage claim that satisfies Constitution Rule 5.
- Never identify a Tier 4 source.
- Never convert a Likely route or Observed Pattern into a confirmed opening.
- Never leak paid-only detail.
- Never pad a thread to hit a fixed post count.
- Never use rage hooks, fake questions, vague urgency, or “you won't believe” language.

## Voice

Dry, specific, and professionally useful. The account sounds like a desk that tracks buyers, not a commentator trying to win engagement.

Good:

> Six revenge-romance titles appeared in ReelShort's catalog this week. The additions are evidence of active appetite; underlying commission terms are not confirmed.

Bad:

> Hollywood has no idea what's coming next — and these six secret shows prove it.

## Procedure

1. Read the signed free edition once.
2. Identify the cold open, confirmed money moves, labeled signals, and strongest context item.
3. Draft the X thread:
   - opener: compress the cold open into one precise sentence;
   - middle: one signed item per post, preserving its classification;
   - context: optional, only if it adds real fluency;
   - final post: one accurate CTA.
4. Draft LinkedIn as a short analysis:
   - what happened;
   - what the evidence supports;
   - why a working producer, packager, representative, or BD lead should care;
   - one CTA.
5. Draft optional drip posts only from signed items not already exhausted by the main thread.
6. Deliver all copy in one review block with source record IDs retained for the human reviewer.

## Templates

### X opener

```text
[Buyer or market actor] [precise deal- or signal-grade verb] [development].
[One sentence on why it changes the current buyer map].
```

### X item

```text
[Buyer] [classification-appropriate verb] [what].
[Disclosed scale or “value not disclosed”]. Why it matters: [one supported clause].
```

### LinkedIn opener

```text
[Buyer] [precise development]. The important part is not the headline; it is what the move says about [specific commissioning, financing, distribution, or mandate shift].
```

### CTA

```text
The Pickup's Deal Board tracks current buyers and mandates with source links, confidence labels, and verified action routes. [link]
```

Do not use “every buyer,” “every mandate,” or “fully verified.” The database is selective by design, and mandate confidence varies.

## Human review

- [ ] Every claim and number appears in the signed free edition.
- [ ] Deal and signal verbs match the signed classifications.
- [ ] No unsupported “trades missed this” or exclusivity language appears.
- [ ] No paid-only detail or Tier 4 source is exposed.
- [ ] Action-route and mandate-confidence labels remain intact.
- [ ] The thread length reflects the available material rather than a quota.
- [ ] The voice is specific and credible to a working professional.
- [ ] Each platform contains one CTA, no more.
